That makes total sense. Right now the tabs are inconsistent across rails:

Rail 1 (Stripe Wallet): Wallets | Activity | Approvals
Rail 2 (Card Wallet): Wallets | Activity | Orders | Approvals
Rail 4/5 (Cards): No tabs — just a card list
And the naming is muddled. "Activity" on Rail 1 shows transactions. "Activity" on Rail 2 shows transactions and some order info mixed together. "Orders" only exists on Rail 2.

Your distinction is clean and correct:

Transactions = the financial ledger. Amount, timestamp, status (pending/confirmed/failed), direction (in/out), balance after. This is the money view. Every rail has this — even card rails have debits and deposits.

Orders = the procurement record. Vendor, product name, product link, price, quantity, shipping status, tracking info. This is the "what did I buy" view. Today only Rail 2 has this, but once procurement is modularized (Phase 2), any rail could have orders.

So the unified tab set should be something like:

Tab	What it shows	Which rails have it
Wallets (or Cards)	The wallet/card list with action bars	All
Transactions	Financial ledger — deposits, debits, transfers, refunds	All
Orders	Procurement records — products, vendors, shipping, tracking	Rail 2 today, any rail with procurement later
Approvals	Pending approval queue	All (any rail can have approval thresholds)
This should go into the plan as a follow-on task after the wallet UI refactor (T007 area) — extracting the tab structure into a shared component in components/wallet/ that accepts config for which tabs to show. The tab content components (transaction list, order list, approval queue) become shared too, with rail-specific data fetching passed in as props or hooks.
